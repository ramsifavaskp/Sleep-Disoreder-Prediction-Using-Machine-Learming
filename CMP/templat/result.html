    Consult a Healthcare Professional: Seek medical advice if sleep apnea symptoms such as loud snoring, gasping for air during sleep, or excessive daytime sleepiness persist.
    Consider Lifestyle Changes: Maintain a healthy weight, as obesity is a risk factor for sleep apnea. Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption.

    Sleep Position: Sleep on your side rather than your back to reduce the risk of obstructive sleep apnea.
    Elevate Head During Sleep: Prop up the head with pillows or use a wedge pillow to elevate it slightly, which may help alleviate symptoms of sleep apnea.
